## Anomalies in the Relational Model

Anomalies in the relational model refer to inconsistencies or errors that can arise when working with relational databases, especially during data insertion, deletion, and modification. These anomalies can compromise data integrity and make database management inefficient.

### Causes of Anomalies in DBMS

Anomalies are typically caused by:

- Poor management of storing all data in a flat database
- Lack of normalization
- Data redundancy
- Improper use of primary or foreign keys

### Types of Anomalies

1. **Insertion Anomalies**  
   Occur when it is not possible to insert data because required fields are missing or data is incomplete.  
   _Example:_ If a record requires a primary key but none is provided, it cannot be inserted.

2. **Deletion Anomalies**  
   Occur when deleting a record unintentionally removes additional data.  
   _Example:_ Deleting a customer may also delete all orders associated with that customer.

3. **Update Anomalies**  
   Occur when modifying data leads to inconsistencies or errors.  
   _Example:_ Updating an employee's salary in one record but not in all related records can cause incorrect calculations and reporting.

> **Note:** These anomalies can be minimized or eliminated through normalization, which restructures the database to reduce redundancy and improve data integrity.
