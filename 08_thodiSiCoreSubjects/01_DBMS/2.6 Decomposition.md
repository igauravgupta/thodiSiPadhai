## Introduction

Decomposition in DBMS refers to the process of breaking a relation (table) into two or more smaller relations to eliminate redundancy, anomalies, and to achieve normalization.

## Need for Decomposition

- To remove data redundancy
- To avoid update, insertion, and deletion anomalies
- To achieve higher normal forms (like 2NF, 3NF, BCNF)

## Types of Decomposition

### Lossless Decomposition

- Ensures that no information is lost during decomposition.
- The original relation can be reconstructed by joining the decomposed relations.
- **Condition:** At least one decomposed relation must contain a candidate key of the original relation.

### Lossy Decomposition

- Some information may be lost after decomposition.
- The original relation cannot be perfectly reconstructed.
- Generally avoided in practice.

## Properties of Decomposition

- **Preservation of Dependencies:** Functional dependencies should be preserved after decomposition.
- **Lossless Join:** The join of decomposed relations should result in the original relation.

## Examples

### Example of Lossless Decomposition

Suppose we have a relation:
| StudentID | Name | Course | Instructor |
|-----------|------|--------|------------|

Decompose into:

- R1(StudentID, Name)
- R2(StudentID, Course, Instructor)

Joining R1 and R2 on StudentID gives the original relation.

### Example of Lossy Decomposition

If we decompose the above relation into:

- R1(Name, Course)
- R2(Course, Instructor)

Joining R1 and R2 may produce spurious tuples, leading to loss of information.

## Advantages & Disadvantages

### Advantages

- Eliminates redundancy
- Removes anomalies
- Simplifies data structure

### Disadvantages

- May lead to complex queries
- Possible loss of dependencies

## Conclusion

Decomposition is a crucial concept in DBMS for designing efficient and normalized databases. Proper decomposition ensures data integrity and eliminates anomalies.

### Difference Between Decomposition and Normalization

| Aspect                  | Decomposition                                                       | Normalization                                                         |
| ----------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Definition              | Breaking a relation into two or more smaller relations              | Process of organizing data to reduce redundancy and improve integrity |
| Purpose                 | To eliminate redundancy and anomalies, and to achieve normalization | To achieve higher normal forms and minimize redundancy                |
| Process                 | Splitting tables based on attributes and dependencies               | Applying normal forms (1NF, 2NF, 3NF, BCNF, etc.) step by step        |
| Result                  | Smaller relations that may be joined to reconstruct the original    | Relations in higher normal forms with reduced anomalies               |
| Dependency Preservation | May or may not preserve all functional dependencies                 | Focuses on preserving dependencies while achieving normal forms       |
| Lossless Property       | Should ideally be lossless to avoid loss of information             | Ensures lossless join as part of normalization steps                  |
